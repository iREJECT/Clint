Ramirez
elected ARSC
Provincial prenxy

Dannah Jane Gargar

Consolatrician Cybill Ann Ramirez won as the ARSC Provincial Chapter President of the Province of Mo.Cecilia last September 11-14 2013,during the 8th ARSC Provincial Chapter Congress,with the theme: "Integral faith and community building" held at St.Ezekiel Development Center, Puerto Princesa Palawan. Ramirez bested nine other candidates from the other AR schools composting: Blessed Trinity College-talibon,Bohol;Immaculate Heart of Mary Academy-Minglanilla, Cebu; St. Augustine Academy - Bayawan City, Negros Oriental;St.Mary Academy-Guindulman, Bohol;St.Paul Academy-Bantayan Island, Cebu;Sta. Monica Academy-Pinamungahan,Cebu; Colegio de Sta.Teresita-Laoang,Northern Samar; Colegio De La Medalla Milagrosa-Jagna, Bohol; and Colegio de Sta. Rita-San Carlos City,negros Occidental. Ramirez and the candidates pledged their oath of office during the last day of the congress,after the thanksgiving mass. The four-day Provincial Congress is held after every years to strengthen the bond and leadership of every ARSCian through team building activities and leadership trainings. Though the provincial Congress came to an end, and the ARSCians may have parted their ways,but the spirit and heart of a true ARSCian leader will always remain in each of them.

CC scribes hurdle 38 awards

Mary Katelynne Corbo

Consolatrix College Toledo City campus journalist once again proved thet Consolatricians can stand out in any aspect, as they bagged several awards during the 25th Division Schools Press Conference (DSPC) last November 14-15,2013. "Responsible Campus Journalism:A Call for Truth and Transparency," was the theme that inspired CCTC scribes to gather 38 awards in the said confab, among the 57 schools that joined. This year's DSPC ws held at the Consolatrix College of Toledo City. The CC delegation was composed of 28 elementary and 28 high school students. They completed in seven categories, namely:News Writing,Feature Writing,Editorial Writing,Editorial Cartooning,Copyreading and Photo Journalism both in English and Filipino. With prayers and hardworks, CCTC journalists bagged the following awards: SECONDARY-OFFICIAL WINNERS: Steffi Honoridez 2nd Copyreading and Headline Writing (Filipino)
